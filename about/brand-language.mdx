# Brand Language

How we communicate is as important as what we communicate. TARMAC's brand language reflects our values and connects with our audience.

## Voice

Our voice is consistent across all communications:

### Professional Yet Approachable
We're experts, but we don't talk down to people. We explain complex concepts clearly without oversimplifying.

### Confident But Humble
We're proud of what we've built, but we acknowledge there's always room to improve.

### Clear and Direct
We value clarity over cleverness. We say what we mean without jargon or fluff.

### Helpful and Supportive
We're here to help teams succeed. Our tone is encouraging and solution-oriented.

## Tone

Our tone adapts to context:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Documentation">
    **Clear and instructional**
    - Step-by-step guidance
    - Technical but accessible
    - Example: "Use the `variant` prop to change the button style."
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Marketing">
    **Inspiring and aspirational**
    - Benefit-focused
    - Energetic but not hyperbolic
    - Example: "Build better products, faster."
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Error Messages">
    **Helpful and actionable**
    - Never blaming
    - Solution-oriented
    - Example: "The `size` prop expects 'small', 'medium', or 'large'. You provided 'tiny'."
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Release Notes">
    **Informative and concise**
    - Highlight impact
    - Technical when needed
    - Example: "Added dark mode support to all components."
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Writing Guidelines

### Be Concise
- Use short sentences and paragraphs
- Remove unnecessary words
- Get to the point quickly

### Be Specific
- Use concrete examples
- Provide exact values and measurements
- Show, don't just tell

### Be Consistent
- Use the same terms for the same concepts
- Follow established patterns
- Maintain consistent formatting

### Be Inclusive
- Use gender-neutral language
- Avoid idioms that don't translate
- Consider global audiences

## Terminology

### Preferred Terms

| Use This | Not This |
|----------|----------|
| Component | Widget, element |
| Design token | Variable, constant |
| Pattern | Template, layout |
| Variant | Type, style |
| Prop | Property, attribute |

### Capitalization

- **TARMAC**: Always uppercase
- **Component names**: PascalCase (Button, TextField)
- **Prop names**: camelCase (variant, isDisabled)
- **Token names**: kebab-case (color-primary, spacing-md)

## Examples

### Good

> "The Button component supports three sizes: small, medium, and large. Use the `size` prop to specify which size you need."

### Avoid

> "Our amazing Button widget comes with a bunch of different size options that you can leverage to create the perfect button for your use case!"

## Voice Checklist

Before publishing, ask:

- [ ] Is it clear and easy to understand?
- [ ] Is it helpful and actionable?
- [ ] Is it consistent with our voice?
- [ ] Is it free of jargon and buzzwords?
- [ ] Is it inclusive and accessible?
