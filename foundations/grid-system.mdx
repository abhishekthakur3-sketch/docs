## Overview

A grid system is the invisible structural framework that organizes layout, spacing, and alignment across your product. It ensures consistency, predictability, and rhythm — making interfaces feel balanced, intuitive, and easy to scan. A strong grid acts like a "traffic system" for UI elements: guiding where components should sit, how they scale, and how they respond across breakpoints.

## Types of Grid

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Fluid Grid">
    A fluid grid stretches across the screen to fill all available space. Columns and content scale and resize proportionally with the viewport.

    **When to use:**
    - Responsive layouts that need to adapt to any screen width
    - Content-heavy pages where maximizing reading area matters
    - Dashboards and data-dense interfaces

    <Info>Fluid grids are the default for most TARMAC layouts. They ensure content fills the available space while maintaining column proportions.</Info>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Fixed Grid">
    A fixed grid applies an ideal maximum width to page containers. Content is centered within the viewport, and the grid does not stretch beyond its defined max-width.

    **Variants:**
    - **Fixed Narrow** — For focused reading experiences and forms
    - **Fixed Wide** — For content-rich layouts with sidebars

    **When to use:**
    - Marketing and landing pages where line length control matters
    - Form-heavy workflows that benefit from constrained width
    - Screens where content readability is more important than space utilization

    <Tip>On viewports wider than 1440px, the TARMAC grid locks to a 1280px centered container to prevent eye-strain from overly long line lengths.</Tip>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Column Span & Offset

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Column Span">
    Column span defines how many columns a content block occupies. Spans range from 2 to 12 columns on desktop, allowing flexible layout compositions.

    | Span | Desktop Width (approx.) | Common Use |
    |------|------------------------|------------|
    | 2 cols | ~16% | Sidebar icons, small widgets |
    | 3 cols | ~25% | Side panels, navigation |
    | 4 cols | ~33% | Cards, form sections |
    | 6 cols | ~50% | Split layouts, two-column content |
    | 8 cols | ~66% | Main content with sidebar |
    | 12 cols | 100% | Full-width sections, heroes |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Offset">
    Offset adds empty space by skipping columns before a content block. This creates visual balance and asymmetric layouts without adding empty containers.

    **Use offsets to:**
    - Center content within the grid
    - Create visual breathing room
    - Align content blocks with other sections on the page
    - Build asymmetric but intentional layouts

    <Warning>Avoid excessive offsets that push content too far from the viewport edge. Users should not have to scan across large empty areas to find content.</Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Desktop Grid System

### Supported Viewports

| Screen Size | Usage |
|-------------|-------|
| 1440 × 942px | Primary design frame and baseline grid |
| 1366 × 942 | Widespread enterprise resolution |
| 1280 × 832 | Compact laptop and workspace views |
| 1920+ | Grid locked to 1280px, centered |

### Content Grid

The base 12-column content grid used across all desktop breakpoints.

| Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Column Width | Type |
|------------|----------|---------|--------|--------------|------|
| Large | 1920 × 1080 | 12 | 24px | 92px | Centre |
| Default | 1440 × 942 | 12 | 16px | 92px | Centre |
| Medium | 1366 × 768 | 12 | 16px | 92px | Centre |
| Small | 1280 × 832 | 12 | 16px | 92px | Centre |

<Info>The content grid is always 12 columns and centre-aligned. Column width remains consistent at 92px — only gutters change between Large (24px) and other breakpoints (16px).</Info>

### Layout Contexts

Desktop layouts adapt the grid based on navigation context. Each context adjusts column widths and offsets to accommodate navigation elements while preserving alignment.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Side Navigation — Expanded" icon="sidebar">
    When the side navigation is expanded, the content grid reduces proportionally. The side nav occupies a 4-column equivalent area.

    | Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Column Width | Offset | Type |
    |------------|----------|---------|--------|--------------|--------|------|
    | Large | 1920 × 1080 | 12 | 24px | 76px | 24px | Centre |
    | Default | 1440 × 942 | 12 | 16px | 76px | 24px | Right |
    | Medium | 1366 × 768 | 12 | 16px | 72px | 38px | Right |
    | Small | 1280 × 832 | 12 | 16px | 68px | 24px | Right |
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Side Navigation — Collapsed" icon="sidebar-flip">
    When the side navigation is collapsed, the content grid expands while maintaining alignment with the collapsed nav rail.

    | Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Column Width | Offset | Type |
    |------------|----------|---------|--------|--------------|--------|------|
    | Large | 1920 × 1080 | 12 | 24px | 94px | 24px | Centre |
    | Default | 1440 × 942 | 12 | 16px | 94px | 24px | Right |
    | Medium | 1366 × 768 | 12 | 16px | 88px | 38px | Right |
    | Small | 1280 × 832 | 12 | 16px | 82px | 24px | Right |
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Top & Bottom Navigation" icon="bars">
    Grid anchors below the header and above the footer. The full viewport width is available for the content grid.

    | Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Column Width | Type |
    |------------|----------|---------|--------|--------------|------|
    | Large | 1920 × 1080 | 1 | 16px | 80px | Top |
    | Default | 1440 × 942 | 1 | 16px | 80px | Top |
    | Medium | 1366 × 768 | 1 | 16px | 72px | Top |
    | Small | 1280 × 832 | 1 | 16px | 68px | Top |
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Side Drawer" icon="window-restore">
    Drawers overlay or compress the content area. TARMAC supports 4-column (standard) and 6-column (extended) drawer widths.

    | Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Column Width | Offset | Type |
    |------------|----------|---------|--------|--------------|--------|------|
    | SD_L (1440) | 1440 × 942 | 4 | 16px | 86px | 24px | Right |
    | SD_XL (1440) | 1440 × 942 | 6 | 16px | 82px | 24px | Right |
    | SD_L (1366) | 1366 × 768 | 4 | 16px | 64px | 24px | Right |
    | SD_XL (1366) | 1366 × 768 | 6 | 16px | 60px | 24px | Right |
    | SD_L (1280) | 1280 × 832 | 4 | 16px | 60px | 24px | Right |
    | SD_XL (1280) | 1280 × 832 | 6 | 16px | 56px | 24px | Right |

    <Tip>Use the 4-column drawer (SD_L) for simple forms and detail panels. Reserve the 6-column drawer (SD_XL) for complex workflows that need more horizontal space.</Tip>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Accessibility & Readability

Desktop grid decisions directly impact readability and accessibility. The TARMAC grid is designed to:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Maintain consistent reading widths">
    Content stays within a 1440px grid frame to prevent eye-strain on ultra-wide monitors.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prevent excessive line lengths">
    On screens wider than 1920px, the grid locks to a centered 1280px container.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Support predictable scan patterns">
    Consistent column alignment creates reliable visual anchors for high-density UIs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preserve typographic hierarchy">
    Grid structure reinforces heading and body text relationships through consistent alignment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ensure comfortable interaction spacing">
    Gutters and margins provide adequate spacing for motor interactions and touch targets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Balance contrast and whitespace">
    The grid prevents content from becoming too dense or too sparse at any breakpoint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Mobile Grid System

### Supported Viewports

| Screen Size | Usage |
|-------------|-------|
| 390 × 844 | Default reference size |
| 360 × 800 | Secondary modern devices |
| 320 × 568 | POS and constrained environments |

### Main Content Grid

The mobile content grid uses 4 columns across all breakpoints, providing a simple and consistent layout framework.

| Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Margin |
|------------|----------|---------|--------|--------|
| Large | 390 × 844 | 4 | 16px | 16px |
| Default | 360 × 800 | 4 | 16px | 16px |
| Small | 320 × 568 | 4 | 16px | 16px |

<Info>Mobile gutters and margins remain fixed at 16px across all breakpoints. The columns themselves are fluid and resize to fill the available width.</Info>

### Navigation Grids

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Top & Bottom Nav (2 Columns)">
    The navigation bar uses a simplified 2-column grid for balanced placement of navigation elements.

    | Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Offset |
    |------------|----------|---------|--------|--------|
    | Large | 390 × 844 | 2 | 16px | 24 & 16px |
    | Default | 360 × 800 | 2 | 16px | 24 & 16px |
    | Small | 320 × 568 | 2 | 16px | 16 & 12px |
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Side Nav (3 Columns)">
    The mobile side navigation overlay uses a 3-column grid for menu items and navigation structure.

    | Breakpoint | Viewport | Columns | Gutter | Offset |
    |------------|----------|---------|--------|--------|
    | Large | 390 × 844 | 3 | 16px | 16px |
    | Default | 360 × 800 | 3 | 16px | 16px |
    | Small | 320 × 568 | 3 | 16px | 16px |
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

The 4-column content grid, 2-column navigation grid, and 3-column side navigation grid form a flexible yet disciplined framework that adapts across Large, Default, and Small breakpoints.

## General Principles

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Consistency over complexity" icon="equals">
    Apply the same visual logic across all surfaces. A predictable grid reduces cognitive load for both users and builders.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Content-first alignment" icon="align-left">
    Grids support meaning — they don't compete with it. Layout decisions should serve the content hierarchy, not override it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Predictable navigation zones" icon="compass">
    Users should always know where to look. Consistent placement of navigation, content, and actions builds spatial memory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Scalable spacing system" icon="ruler-combined">
    Built from TARMAC spacing tokens. Grid gutters, margins, and offsets reference the same scale used across all components.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Clarity at operational scale" icon="binoculars">
    Interfaces must remain readable in real-world environments — from compact POS terminals to wide enterprise dashboards.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Grid stability" icon="lock">
    Layouts should not jump, shift, or realign unexpectedly. Transitions between breakpoints and navigation states must feel smooth and intentional.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>Never break the grid to "make something fit." If content doesn't align to the grid, revisit the content structure or component sizing — not the grid itself.</Warning>
